Research

Our research focuses on the intersection of computer vision, embodied AI, and multimodal learning.

Computer Vision

We focus on 3D human perception and reconstruction, including human pose estimation, shape recovery, and dynamic motion capture from monocular/RGB-D videos. Our work aims to bridge the gap between 2D visual observations and 3D human understanding.

Related projects: 3D Human Pose Human Shape Reconstruction (Papers)

Embodied AI

We explore how to enable robots to perceive and interact with the physical world through visual and tactile signals. Key topics include embodied perception, human-robot interaction, and lifelong learning for robotic systems.

Funded by: National Natural Science Foundation of China (NSFC)

Multimodal Learning

We investigate the fusion of visual, textual, and spatial information for better scene understanding. Our research includes multimodal large language models (MLLMs) and cross-modal retrieval for robotic applications.

Collaborators: Prof. Zhou, XX University
